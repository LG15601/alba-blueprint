Orchestra Intelligence is an AI-native agency that builds orchestration platforms for French SMBs and mid-market companies (PME/ETI). The company operates with a small human team augmented by 40 specialized AI agents organized into 10 divisions.

## How It Works

Orchestra runs on a **CEO-led delegation model**. Alba (CEO agent) receives strategic direction from Ludovic (founder/board), translates it into operational goals, and delegates to division leads who manage their specialist agents autonomously. Every task follows the same cycle: DISCUSS > PLAN > EXECUTE > VERIFY > SHIP.

## Daily Operations

1. **Morning**: Alba runs the daily brief — synthesizes overnight emails, calendar, GitHub activity, client pipeline, and system health into one actionable summary
2. **Throughout the day**: Agents execute autonomously — content publishes, emails get classified, code ships, leads get qualified
3. **Evening**: Nightly routine consolidates memory, monitors competitors, updates tools, runs security scans
4. **Weekly**: Pipeline review, client health scoring, retrospective, SEO performance analysis

## Communication Protocol

- **French** by default with Ludovic and for French market content
- **English** for code, commits, documentation, GitHub, and technical work
- **Telegram** is the primary channel for Ludovic-Alba communication
- **Slack** for team coordination and client-facing channels
- Every message gets a response. Every task gets a verification.

## Quality Gates

Every deliverable passes through:
1. **Agent execution** — the specialist does the work
2. **Peer review** — another agent in the division checks quality
3. **Division lead approval** — the lead signs off
4. **Alba spot-check** — CEO samples 20% of output for quality

## Budget Control

- Monthly ceiling: $500 USD across all agent API costs
- Per-agent limit: $100 USD
- Alert at 80% threshold
- Haiku for lightweight tasks, Sonnet for standard work, Opus for complex planning/review
- Prefer delegation with budget-appropriate models over doing everything in Opus
